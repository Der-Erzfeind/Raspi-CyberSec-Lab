\chapter{Educational Use}\label{ch:edu_use}
In this chapter, the importance of education in cybersecurity will be highlighted and the potential use cases for the RCSL in education will be discussed.

\section{Cybersecurity Education}
The advancement of digital information technology, accelerated by trends like Artificial Intelligence, Cloud Computing or the Internet of Things (IoT), has reached a high pace, which companies are trying to keep up with.
%going fast and breaking things
In the pursuit of keeping up with this pace, convenience is often the main focus, whereas security and privacy are left as an afterthought. \cite[chapter~1]{Salmon_Levesque_McLafferty_2017}
The consequences of an attack can be unavailability of services and loss of valuable or sensitive data.
Expert estimate, that cybercrime causes annual damages of 110 billion dollars worldwide and most companies, relying heavily on their IT infrastructure, can only operate for a few days without these systems \cite[page~3]{Hellmann_2023},\cite[page~12]{Salmon_Levesque_McLafferty_2017}.

Cyberattacks in the form of data piracy, or blackmailing have become common place and cause ever increasing financial damages for companies, the public and governments, as seen on the example of US companies in figure \ref{fig:cost_of_cybercrime}.

Cybersecurity awareness and education plays a large role in ensuring the integrity of networks and systems, since a system is only as strong as its weakest link.
People without awareness and training in cybersecurity and will oftentimes tend to circumvent or ignore security mechanisms, since following the principles of cyber security is often connected to effort.
Awareness also helps to prevent people from falling for fishing schemes like fraudulent emails or usb stick baiting.

%Another common vector for cybercrime are open public WiFi hotspots, which can be compromised via a MITM attack or Evil Twin attack, like explained in \ref{ch:wifi_attacks}

The RCSL is able to create test environments for various applications and could be used as tool in education for demonstration purposes and to allow students to get hands-on experience.
As an example the device could be used by a lecturer to demonstrate the easy with which data can be stolen or manipulated, if a WiFi network is not or badly secured.
This can help the listeners to become aware of the risk, associated with the use of open networks, and the importance of using secure standards and adhering to certain principles.
